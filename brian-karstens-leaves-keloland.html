Longtime Anchor Brian Karstens Departs KELOLAND News

Sioux Falls, SD - After a significant tenure serving viewers across South Dakota and the surrounding region, veteran news anchor Brian Karstens has announced his departure from KELOLAND News. The news marks a notable change in the local broadcast journalism landscape, where Karstens has been a familiar face for many years.

Brian Karstens was a prominent figure at KELOLAND Media Group, known for delivering the news with professionalism and a connection to the community. Throughout his time at the station, he covered countless stories that impacted the lives of residents, from local politics and community events to significant weather patterns and statewide developments. His consistent presence on evening newscasts helped build trust and familiarity with the audience, making him a respected voice in Sioux Falls news and beyond.

Details surrounding his reasons for leaving and his future plans have been emerging, with KELOLAND and Karstens himself sharing statements regarding the transition. While specific next steps for Karstens may vary, his departure signals the end of a particular era for the KELOLAND news desk.

For viewers who relied on his reporting, his absence will undoubtedly be felt. Karstens' dedication to covering important issues and keeping the public informed was a hallmark of his career at the station. He navigated the complexities of daily news production, contributing significantly to KELOLAND's role as a primary source for local information in the region.

As KELOLAND adapts its news team following this departure, the focus remains on continuing to provide comprehensive local coverage. Meanwhile, the community reflects on Brian Karstens' contributions to broadcast journalism in South Dakota and wishes him well in his future endeavors. His time at KELOLAND leaves a legacy of committed reporting and service to the viewers he informed daily.
